I wanted to get experience on the investment side of tech for two reasons. First, I wanted to learn about a ton of different startups so I have context for when I work on startups. Apple

$2.4T Market Cap • 36,786 Employees

Apple was probably my favorite internship. I joined a computer vision team working to combine muliple camera views for object detection. At the conclusion of my project, I had improved accuracy by an average of 31%. I also got to compete in the iContest (pitching features and products to Apple on a team of interns), hackathons, presentations, and poster symposiums. Surprisingly my biggest gain was learning the value of good presenting and how to build a great presentation.

Microsoft

$2.2T Market Cap • 182,268 Employees

Joining Microsoft for a summer internship was one of my best decisions. I received phenomenal mentorship and learned how big tech is managed and run. I worked on Microsoft Azure, their cloud platform. While I was there I built 3 APIs to let customers know which versions they can upgrade or downgrade their clusters to. These APIs serviced 1,600+ customers and resolved daily customer service calls. I worked remotely, which was a bit of a bummer. In the end I received an offer to return fulltime, but I decided to turn it down to pursue opportunities outside of software engineering.
