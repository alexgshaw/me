How Will You Measure Your Life?

Author: Clayton Christensen

Rating: 9/10 • Topic: Self-Improvement

This is one of the most influencial books that I have read. Clayton Christensen talks about how to find meaningful ways to measure your life. My main takeaway is that just because success in work is more measurable than success in your personal life, it is not more valuable. So take time to spend with family and friends.

Principles

Author: Ray Dalio

Rating: 10/10 • Topic: Productivity

Principles changed my perspective more than any other book I have read. Ray Dalio talks about systemizing decisions, being open-minded, evaluating sources, and questioning others' believability. He explains that the best results in life come from deciding what your principles are and then using them to make your decisions.

Zero to One

Author: Peter Thiel

Rating: 9/10 • Topic: Startups

I read this book when I started my venture capital job to get a better feel for what makes startups successful. It's a quick read and I found it very engaging and informative. Peter Thiel talks about what distinguishes great businesses and how to create them.

Why We Sleep

Author: Matthew Walker

Rating: 7/10 • Topic: Health

I read this book because I wanted to learn more about how sleep affects health. The first couple chapters were really good and I felt like I learned good information that changed my views and understanding about sleep. After that, my level of interest dropped. I would recommend watching his TED talk before recommending you read his book.
